Bridging the Gap: From Classroom to Capital

The U.S. federal government faces a critical shortage of cybersecurity talent, particularly in roles requiring handling of sensitive data.

Located in the heart of the National Capital Region, The Sentry Initiative serves as a direct feeder system. We identify high-potential K-12 students and provide them with the specific "Cloud Sovereignty" and "AI Safety" skills required by agencies such as CISA, NSA, and the Dept. of Energy.

Academic Curriculum

Moving beyond basic IT literacy to specialized defense doctrines.

Module 01

Cloud Data Sovereignty

Students learn to architect "Data Vaults" that segregate sensitive government data (FedRAMP standards) from public internet access.

  • VPC Architecture & Network Isolation
  • Identity Access Management (IAM)
Module 02

AI Counter-Adversarial Operations

Applied study of the MITRE ATLAS framework. Students learn to defend AI models against poisoning and evasion attacks.

  • Model Robustness Testing
  • Algorithmic Bias Detection
Module 03

LLM Governance (OWASP)

Focusing on the secure deployment of Large Language Models within government agencies, adhering to OWASP Top 10 for LLMs.

  • Prompt Injection Mitigation
  • Data Leakage Prevention
Capstone

Infrastructure Defense Simulations

Students are deployed into simulated Sector Teams to defend critical assets against nation-state actors.
